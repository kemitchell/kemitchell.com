Kyle E. Mitchell

After a stint in Moscow, Russia studying software-copyright law and politics, he returned to Texas for graduate studies, and eventually law school. Out of law school, Kyle joined the small Silicon Valley office of a large law firm, serving start-up to large technology companies with corporate, commercial, and intellectual property advice. The firm made him use Microsoft Windows. The firm would not let him use Dropbox.

Along the way, he's released several permissively licensed contract forms, a suite of tools for contract preparation, automation, and sharing, popular tooling for automated license metadata management and audit in the JavaScript ecosystem, and a distributed prior-art publication for securing scientific findings for the public domain. He's launched two legal technology utility companies, one for contract standardization and electronic signature, another for microscale commercial dual licensing of open software. He's had a few hits on the blog, mostly writing on open source software and legal technology, with the occasional dash of blockchain.
